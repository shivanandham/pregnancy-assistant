PREGNANCY DO'S AND DON'TS GUIDE
=====================================

NUTRITION DO'S:
- Eat a balanced diet with plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins
- Take prenatal vitamins daily, especially folic acid (400-800 mcg)
- Stay hydrated - drink 8-10 glasses of water daily
- Eat small, frequent meals to help with nausea
- Include iron-rich foods like spinach, lean meat, and beans
- Consume calcium-rich foods like dairy, leafy greens, and fortified foods
- Eat foods high in omega-3 fatty acids (salmon, walnuts, flaxseeds)
- Choose whole grains over refined grains
- Eat plenty of fiber to prevent constipation
- Include protein in every meal and snack

NUTRITION DON'TS:
- Don't eat raw or undercooked meat, fish, or eggs
- Don't consume unpasteurized dairy products or soft cheeses
- Don't eat high-mercury fish (shark, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish)
- Don't drink alcohol - no amount is considered safe
- Don't consume excessive caffeine (limit to 200mg/day)
- Don't eat deli meats unless heated until steaming hot
- Don't consume raw sprouts
- Don't eat foods with artificial sweeteners in excess
- Don't skip meals or severely restrict calories
- Don't take herbal supplements without doctor approval

EXERCISE DO'S:
- Get regular, moderate exercise (30 minutes most days)
- Choose low-impact activities like walking, swimming, prenatal yoga
- Do pelvic floor exercises (Kegels) daily
- Practice good posture and body mechanics
- Stay active throughout pregnancy unless advised otherwise
- Listen to your body and rest when needed
- Stay cool and hydrated during exercise
- Wear supportive, comfortable clothing and shoes
- Focus on maintaining fitness, not gaining new skills
- Include gentle stretching in your routine

EXERCISE DON'TS:
- Don't start new high-intensity exercise programs
- Don't exercise in hot, humid conditions
- Don't do exercises that involve lying flat on your back after 16 weeks
- Don't participate in contact sports or activities with fall risk
- Don't hold your breath during exercises (Valsalva maneuver)
- Don't exercise to the point of exhaustion
- Don't do exercises that cause pain or discomfort
- Don't scuba dive or participate in high-altitude activities
- Don't do exercises that involve twisting at the waist
- Don't ignore warning signs like dizziness or chest pain

LIFESTYLE DO'S:
- Get 7-9 hours of sleep per night
- Sleep on your left side in the third trimester
- Practice stress management techniques (meditation, deep breathing)
- Maintain good hygiene and oral care
- Wear comfortable, supportive clothing and shoes
- Use pregnancy-safe skincare products
- Keep regular prenatal appointments
- Educate yourself about pregnancy and childbirth
- Prepare your home for the baby's arrival
- Build a support network of family and friends
- Practice relaxation techniques
- Stay informed about pregnancy changes and milestones

LIFESTYLE DON'TS:
- Don't smoke or use tobacco products
- Don't use recreational drugs
- Don't expose yourself to secondhand smoke
- Don't use hot tubs, saunas, or take very hot baths
- Don't use harsh chemicals or cleaning products without ventilation
- Don't lift heavy objects (over 25 pounds)
- Don't stand for long periods without breaks
- Don't wear tight clothing that restricts circulation
- Don't ignore signs of complications
- Don't stress about every little change or symptom
- Don't compare your pregnancy to others
- Don't neglect your mental health

HEALTHCARE DO'S:
- Attend all scheduled prenatal appointments
- Keep track of your symptoms and concerns
- Ask questions during appointments
- Follow your healthcare provider's recommendations
- Get recommended vaccinations (flu, Tdap)
- Monitor your blood pressure and weight
- Report any concerning symptoms immediately
- Keep a pregnancy journal or app
- Prepare questions before appointments
- Discuss birth plan preferences with your provider
- Get regular dental checkups
- Consider genetic testing options

HEALTHCARE DON'TS:
- Don't skip prenatal appointments
- Don't self-diagnose or self-treat symptoms
- Don't take medications without doctor approval
- Don't ignore warning signs or symptoms
- Don't delay seeking medical attention for concerns
- Don't rely solely on internet sources for medical advice
- Don't avoid discussing mental health concerns
- Don't ignore dental problems
- Don't skip recommended tests or screenings
- Don't make major medical decisions without consulting your provider

SAFETY DO'S:
- Always wear a seatbelt properly (lap belt below belly, shoulder belt across chest)
- Use pregnancy-safe household products
- Keep your home well-ventilated
- Practice good food safety and hygiene
- Avoid exposure to harmful chemicals and toxins
- Use proper lifting techniques
- Install smoke detectors and carbon monoxide detectors
- Have an emergency plan and contact information ready
- Keep your phone charged and accessible
- Inform others about your pregnancy in case of emergency

SAFETY DON'TS:
- Don't ride roller coasters or other thrill rides
- Don't participate in activities with high fall risk
- Don't use harsh cleaning chemicals without protection
- Don't handle cat litter (risk of toxoplasmosis)
- Don't garden without gloves (risk of toxoplasmosis)
- Don't paint or use strong chemicals without ventilation
- Don't travel to areas with Zika virus risk
- Don't ignore workplace safety concerns
- Don't use electric blankets or heating pads on high
- Don't expose yourself to X-rays unless medically necessary

MENTAL HEALTH DO'S:
- Acknowledge and express your feelings
- Seek support from loved ones
- Join pregnancy support groups
- Practice mindfulness and relaxation
- Maintain social connections
- Set realistic expectations
- Celebrate milestones and achievements
- Prepare for postpartum changes
- Consider therapy if needed
- Practice self-compassion
- Focus on what you can control
- Build a strong support system

MENTAL HEALTH DON'TS:
- Don't ignore feelings of depression or anxiety
- Don't isolate yourself from others
- Don't compare yourself to other pregnant women
- Don't feel guilty about normal pregnancy emotions
- Don't ignore signs of prenatal depression
- Don't feel pressured to be "perfect"
- Don't avoid seeking help when needed
- Don't bottle up concerns or fears
- Don't let others minimize your feelings
- Don't neglect your emotional well-being

IMPORTANT REMINDERS:
- Every pregnancy is unique - what's normal for one person may not be for another
- Always consult your healthcare provider for personalized advice
- Trust your instincts - you know your body best
- Don't hesitate to ask questions or seek second opinions
- Remember that most pregnancies progress normally
- Focus on what you can do rather than what you can't
- Celebrate this special time in your life
- Prepare for the journey ahead with confidence and support

This guide is for informational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice. Always consult with your healthcare provider for personalized recommendations based on your specific situation.
